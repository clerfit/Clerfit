Welcome to ClerFit

ClerFit is your all-in-one health and fitness website. It helps you calculate, track, and understand important health metrics. Whether it's your body composition, calorie needs, heart health, or fitness level, ClerFit provides easy-to-use calculators and detailed insights.

ClerFit aims to make fitness knowledge accessible to everyone.

BMI (Body Mass Index)

Founder: Adolphe Quetelet

Definition: BMI is a measure of body fat based on your weight relative to your height. It helps determine if you are underweight, normal, overweight, or obese.

Formula: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m))²

Category

CategoryBMI Range
Underweight< 18.5
Normal18.5 – 24.9
Overweight25 – 29.9
Obese30 – 34.9
Severely Obese≥ 35

Types of Health Problems

  • Underweight:
    • Weak immunity
    • Anemia
    • Osteoporosis
    • Nutrient deficiencies
  • Normal:
    • Generally healthy
    • Low risk of disease
  • Overweight:
    • Hypertension
    • Type 2 diabetes
    • Joint pain
    • Stress
  • Obese:
    • Heart disease
    • Stroke
    • High blood pressure
    • Metabolic syndrome
  • Severely Obese:
    • Cardiac arrest risk
    • Severe diabetes
    • Sleep apnea
    • Mobility issues

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)

Founder: Harris & Benedict

Definition: BMR estimates the number of calories your body burns at rest to maintain vital functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production.

Formula:
Men: BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 × weight kg) + (4.799 × height cm) – (5.677 × age years)
Women: BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 × weight kg) + (3.098 × height cm) – (4.330 × age years)

Category

CategoryBMR (kcal/day)
Low< 1200
Average1200 – 1800
High> 1800

Types of Health Problems

  • Low:
    • Fatigue
    • Difficulty gaining weight
    • Low energy
  • Average:
    • Normal metabolism
    • Maintain weight easily
  • High:
    • Harder to gain weight
    • High energy expenditure

Body Fat %

Founder: Deurenberg & others

Definition: Body Fat Percentage measures the proportion of fat in your body relative to total body weight. Helps assess fitness and health risk.

Formula:
Men: 1.20 × BMI + 0.23 × Age – 16.2
Women: 1.20 × BMI + 0.23 × Age – 5.4

Category

CategoryBody Fat %
Essential FatMen: 2–5%, Women: 10–13%
AthletesMen: 6–13%, Women: 14–20%
FitnessMen: 14–17%, Women: 21–24%
AverageMen: 18–24%, Women: 25–31%
ObeseMen: ≥25%, Women: ≥32%

Types of Health Problems

  • Essential Fat:
    • Normal bodily function
    • Low risk of disease
  • Athletes:
    • High fitness
    • Low disease risk
  • Fitness:
    • Good cardiovascular health
  • Average:
    • Moderate health risk
  • Obese:
    • High risk of diabetes
    • Heart disease
    • Hypertension

Ideal Weight

Founder: Devine Formula – B.J. Devine

Definition: Ideal Weight is an estimate of optimal body weight based on height, age, and gender for good health.

Formula:
Men: 50 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5 feet

Formula: Calorie Needs = BMR × Activity Factor

Protein Intake

Founder: Dr. William Rose (Nutritional Research)

Definition: Protein intake is the amount of protein required daily for maintaining muscle, tissue repair, and overall health. It varies with activity level.

Formula: Protein (g/day) = Body Weight (kg) × Activity Factor
Activity Factor: Sedentary 0.8, Active 1.2, Athlete 1.6, Bodybuilding 2.0

Category

Activity LevelProtein g/kg
Sedentary0.8
Active1.2
Athlete1.6
Bodybuilding2.0

Types of Health Problems

  • Low Protein Intake:
    • Muscle loss
    • Fatigue
    • Weakened immunity
    • Slow recovery from injury
  • Normal Intake:
    • Supports muscle maintenance
    • Good metabolism
  • High Intake:
    • Excessive kidney load if uncontrolled
    • Possible dehydration

FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index)

Founder: Kouri et al.

Definition: FFMI measures the amount of muscle mass relative to height, helping assess muscularity independent of body fat.

Formula: FFMI = (Lean Mass in kg ÷ Height² in m²) + 6.1 × (1.8 – Height in m)

RFM (Relative Fat Mass)

Founder: Dr. R. Woolcott et al.

Definition: RFM is a method to estimate body fat percentage using height and waist circumference. It is a simpler alternative to BMI.

Formula:
Men: RFM = 64 – (20 × height ÷ waist)
Women: RFM = 76 – (20 × height ÷ waist)

GenderLowNormalHigh
Male<14%14–24%>24%
Female<23%23–31%>31%

Types of Health Problems

    • Possible hormonal imbalance

    • Low risk of metabolic disease
  • High:
    • Increased risk of cardiovascular disease
    • Type 2 diabetes
    • Hypertension

Formula: VO₂ Max (ml/kg/min) = (Distance in meters – 504.9) ÷ 44.73

Heart Rate

Founder: Early cardiac researchers (Palpitations studies)

Definition: Heart rate is the number of heart beats per minute (bpm). It varies with age, fitness, and activity level.

Formula: Maximum Heart Rate = 220 – Age
Target Heart Rate = 50–85% of Max Heart Rate depending on intensity

Category

CategoryBPM
Bradycardia<60
Normal60–100
Tachycardia>100

Types of Health Problems

  • Normal:
    • Healthy heart function
    • Good cardiovascular fitness
  • Tachycardia:
    • Stress on the heart
    • Increased risk of arrhythmia
    • Possible heart disease

Lean Mass

Founder: Dr. Ancel Keys

Definition: Lean Mass is the total body weight minus fat mass. It includes muscles, bones, organs, and water, essential for metabolism and strength.

Formula: Lean Mass (kg) = Weight – (Weight × Body Fat % ÷ 100)

Formula: ABSI = Waist (m) ÷ (BMI^(2/3) × Height^(1/2))

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

Founder: Dr. James Harris & FAFA

Definition: TDEE is the total calories you burn per day, considering basal metabolism and physical activity.

Formula: TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor
Activity Factors: Sedentary = 1.2, Light = 1.375, Moderate = 1.55, Active = 1.725, Very Active = 1.9

Category

Calories/dayCategory
<1500Low
1500–2500Average
2500–3500High
>3500Very High

Types of Health Problems

  • Low TDEE:
    • Weight gain if calories consumed exceed TDEE
    • Low energy levels
  • Average TDEE:
    • Maintains weight
    • Supports moderate activity
  • High/Very High TDEE:
    • Good for athletes or heavy activity
    • Risk of weight loss if calories are too low

Formula: Calories burned = MET × Weight (kg) × Time (hours)
